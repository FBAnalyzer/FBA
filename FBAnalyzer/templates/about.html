How to use Floorball Scanner

Floorball Scanner gives you insight into your team's performance in real-time during the game. You are able to see how each line performs and take actions based on the data.

Setting teams

After logged in successfully, press Select in the New Game Analysis menu.

Input your team name and opponent team name to the respective text fields.

The game is best analysed when followed from the long side of the field. You should position team buttons so that your team (Team 1) is on the same side as you see it on the field. If your team is on the right side of the field, press the position change button to change sides.

After setting team names and their right position (position can be changed also during the game), you are ready to start. Press the Start Game - button and confirm from the opened window. Notice that you are no longer able to change team names after starting the game.

You are now ready to start analysing. When the game starts, press the Start game - button again to start the game clock. When the period is over and you have analysed the period results, change the next period by pressing Period - button and confirm. Notice that this will reset the period data and you are not able to see the previous period data anymore.

During the game

During the game, your task is to operate game clock, track which line is on field from your team, track on which side the ball is located on the field, which team has the ball, and operate the shot map for both teams.

The game clock is used with the Start/Stop - button. Keeping the game clock updated will enable the correct Time on court and Possession - values. Shot map can be used if the game clock is on or off.

Line on court - buttons are used to track which of your team's line is active. Notice that you only track your own team. In the current version of Floorball Scanner there are three even strength lines, two shorthanded and two powerplay lines. Selected line is indicated with a gray color on the button.

Position - buttons are used to track which team has the ball possession and if the ball possession is on the attack of defence zone, in respect to your team's side of the court.

When your team has the ball, make sure that Team 1 - button is gray (selected). Respectively, if your opponent has the ball, make sure that Team 2 - button is selected.

When the game possession is on your defence zone of the field, make sure that Defence - button is selected. Respectively, if the game possession is on your attack zone, make sure that Attack - button is selected. Notice that play possession should be tracked both when your team and the opponent has the ball possession.

Shot mapping is used to track shots, saves, blocks, misses, and goals for both teams. When a shot takes place, mark the shot location by pressing your finger on the map of the field. From the opened list, select the outcome of the shot (saved, missed, etc.) and you can see a mark being drawn to the map. Color and symbol of the shot will appear based on your selection. Blue color means your team and red color opponent team, respectively. The symbol indicates if the shot was saved (s), blocked (b), missed (m), or goal (g).

Data sheet

One of the best features of Floorball Scanner is that the data is accessible in real-time. Below the input buttons a data sheet can be found that is updated every second. Data is shown for each line of your team and also the team performance is calculated.

You will see two values for every data row separated with a | - sign. The first value is the result from current period, the second value is the result from the whole game.

What do the different data elements mean?

  • Possession = How many percent has your team had the ball
  • Time on Court = Time on court in minutes
  • Att Zone w/no Ball = How many percent has the game possession been on your Attack Zone with / without ball on your team. With = You have the ball on the attack zone. Without = Opponent has the ball and you are backchecking
  • Def Zone w/no Ball = How many percent has the game possession been on your Defence Zone with / without ball on your team. With = You have the ball on the defence zone. Without = Opponent has the ball and you are defending your goal
  • Plus minus = Goals for - goals against
  • Turnovers = Won or lost balls on the defence or attack zone. This is calculated based on Ball possession changes when game clock is on (Team 1 -> Team 2 or Team 2 -> Team 1)
